# Did Trump Try to Overturn the 2020 Election?

You can discuss this topic on [Discord](https://discord.com/channels/1429240627178110988/1491240069619777577)

Typically, after a Presidential election, the losing candidates will concede.
But instead, Trump claimed the [2020 election was a fraud](/topics/elfraud/).
He then took several actions that could have changed the election results and/or prevented Biden from becoming President.

If you think that Trump was justified in these actions because the **2020 election was stolen** from him, then please read this page on [election fraud](/topics/elfraud/) first.

**December 19, 2020:** Trump tweeted an invitation to protesters saying “Big protest in D.C. on January 6th. **Be there, will be wild!**”.
Many of the people who were later charged said that they interpreted this as a call to arms.
[\[CREW\]](https://www.citizensforethics.org/reports-investigations/crew-reports/trump-incited-january-6-defendants/)

**December 30, 2020:** Senator Josh Hawley announced his plan to **object to the certification of the electoral college votes**. Congress was scheduled to certify Joe Biden's win on January 6th.
[\[FoxNews\]](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/josh-hawley-to-object-electoral-college-certification-jan-6),
[\[NPR\]](https://www.npr.org/sections/trump-impeachment-effort-live-updates/2021/01/13/956497657/hawley-defends-decision-to-object-to-electoral-votes)

**January 2, 2021: Trump called Georgia Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger** and tried to pressure him to reverse the Georgia state election results.
Trump made many false claims on the phone call and told Raffensperger "I only need 11,000 votes".
Raffensperger refuted Trump's claims and refused to alter the election results.
Transcript on [\[CNN\]](https://www.cnn.com/2021/01/03/politics/trump-brad-raffensperger-phone-call-transcript/index.html),
Analysis of claims at [\[BrannanCenter\]](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/fact-check-trumps-georgia-call-raffensperger),
[\[Wikipedia\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trump%E2%80%93Raffensperger_phone_call)

**January 5, 2021:** Trump **pressures Mike Pence** to decertify the results of the election. Pence says he cannot legally do so.
[\[PBS\]](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trump-insists-that-pence-can-decertify-results)

**January 6, 2021:** Congress was scheduled to ratify the 2020 election results in the afternoon.
But that morning, Trump held a rally at the White House and told protesters "if you don't **fight like hell** you're not going to have a country anymore".
Trump also said: "I know that everyone here will soon be marching over to the Capitol building to **peacefully and patriotically** make your voices heard."
Trump also called on Mike Pence, again, to decertify the election. [\[NPR\]](https://www.npr.org/sections/congress-electoral-college-tally-live-updates/2021/01/06/953998465/trump-calls-on-pence-to-reject-electoral-votes-pence-says-he-wont)
Giuliani called for "trial by combat". [\[CNN\]](https://www.cnn.com/2021/01/18/politics/trump-bannon-stone-giuliani-capitol-riot-invs/index.html)
So there were some mixed messages. [\[Transcript of rally speech on NPR\]](https://www.npr.org/2021/02/10/966396848/read-trumps-jan-6-speech-a-key-part-of-impeachment-trial)

After the rally, the crowd marched to the Capitol and **broke through the police barricades**.
They broke windows and climbed through into the halls of Congress.
The House and Senate were in session trying to ratify the election results. They were forced to flee to safety.
The crowd outside was chanting that Vice President Mike Pence should be hanged. [\[NBCVideo\]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DXnHIJkZZAs)

At one point, during the attack, a crowd broke through a glass door that was protecting members of Congress. A guard, fearing
for their safety shot and killed Ashli Babbitt. [\[Wikipedia\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Killing_of_Ashli_Babbitt)

Trump had tried to attend the protest at Congress but was prevented by his Secret Secret detail.
He then **watched the attack unfold on television**.
Several White House staff members urged Trump to call off his supporters but Trump refused multiple times. [\[NPR\]](https://www.npr.org/2022/06/28/1108343064/trump-didnt-want-to-stop-capitol-attack-former-white-house-aide-testifies)
Eventually Trump urged them to "Go home with love & in peace. Remember this day forever". In the same message, Trump repeated false claims that the election was stolen.
[\[CNBC\]](https://www.cnbc.com/2021/01/06/trump-tells-capitol-rioters-to-go-home-now-but-still-calls-the-election-stolen.html)

By the end of the day 140 officers were injured
[\[WAPost\]](https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/public-safety/police-union-says-140-officers-injured-in-capitol-riot/2021/01/27/60743642-60e2-11eb-9430-e7c77b5b0297_story.html)
and officer Brian Sicknick died. [\[USCP\]](https://www.uscp.gov/about/honoring-our-fallen/officer-brian-sicknick)

**January 7, 2021:** Congress reconvened and voted to certify the 2020 election results declaring Joe Biden the winner. [\[TheHill\]](https://thehill.com/homenews/house/533052-congress-affirms-biden-win-after-rioters-terrorize-capitol/)

**January 13, 2021:** Trump was impeached for incitement of insurrection. The prosecution presented large amounts of evidence including video footage. [\[WAPost\]](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2021/evidence-trump-second-impeachment/)
The defense claimed that Trump's use of the word "fight" was just a figure of speech and was protected by the first amendment.
The trial ended with a vote of 57-43 for conviction, which fell short of the required 2/3rds majority.
[\[Wikipedia\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_impeachment_of_Donald_Trump)

**February 13, 2021:** Mitch McConnell said "Trump is practically and morally responsible for invoking the events of" January 6th. [\[CNN\]](https://www.cnn.com/videos/politics/2021/02/13/mitch-mcconnell-trump-responsible-for-riot-impeachment-acquittal-sot-vpx.cnn)

One could imagine that Trump did not anticipate the level of violence that would occur after the rally.
He may have been angry with the protesters for going too far.
But if that were the case then why did he **pardon over 1500 protesters** who had been tried and convicted?
[\[jan-6.com\]](https://www.jan-6.com/full-list-of-people-pardoned)

Donald Trump, Rudy Giuliani, Mark Meadows and other were **indicted by a grand jury** on charges of conspiring to manipulate the election outcome.
[\[Indictment\]](https://7e074b9c-c955-4bd9-a700-53936aa93a47.filesusr.com/ugd/2cf5f9_e0790657e2dc4bc2bd8675069a6e7911.pdf)
The case was delayed because the prosecuting attorney, Fani Wilis, had a romantic relationship with one of the other prosecutors.
[\[PBS\]](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/georgia-election-interference-case-stalled-a-year-after-trump-and-allies-were-indicted)
Trump was elected before the trial could resume. The case was dropped, not because of lack of evidence, but because of the difficulty of convicting a sitting President.
[\[GeorgiaRecorder\]](https://georgiarecorder.com/2025/11/26/fulton-county-election-interference-case-against-trump-and-his-allies-is-dismissed/)

**Conclusion:** there is strong evidence that Trump tried to overturn the 2020 election results by:
* falsely claiming the election was stolen,
* pressuring election officials to alter the vote,
* and by urging his supporters to block the certification of the vote by Congress.
